# Hero Traits

- Status: draft
- Last Updated: 2026-03-29
- Phase: prototype

## Purpose

This document defines the MVP hero-trait rule for recruited heroes.
The goal is to create light variation at recruit time without exploding data complexity.

## MVP Rules

### Recruit Trait Rule

Every recruited hero should begin with:

- 1 positive trait
- 1 negative trait

This gives each recruit immediate identity and tradeoff value.

### MVP Design Intent

Traits should:

- create roster differentiation
- influence squad selection decisions
- support stories of "good but flawed" recruits
- remain simple enough to author and test

### MVP Simplicity Rule

Trait effects should begin with small, understandable modifiers or narrow rule hooks.
Do not begin with highly combinational passive systems.

## Long-Term Expansion Points

The longer-term direction can evolve toward archetype-based trait pools.
A recommended long-term target is:

- each recruit archetype has a positive trait pool of 3+
- each recruit archetype has a negative trait pool of 3+

This is explicitly future-facing and not required for MVP.

## Relationship to Archetypes

For MVP, recruit archetypes do not need full trait-combinatorics depth.
They only need enough structure to support controlled variety.
Traits should strengthen archetype identity without requiring a huge content matrix.

## Open Questions

- how many total positive and negative traits are needed for MVP variety?
- should traits be purely numeric, partly behavioral, or mixed?
- do traits interact with synergy systems in MVP, or stay mostly independent?
- when should archetype-specific 3+3 trait pools be introduced without destabilizing balance?
