# Progression Premise

- Status: draft
- Last Updated: 2026-03-29
- Phase: concept framing

## Purpose

This document defines the current progression-premise hypothesis for `survival-manager`.
It focuses on why progression exists and how it should support replayability without locking the project into premature system depth.

## Premise Hypothesis

Progression should reinforce return motivation, build experimentation, and a feeling of cumulative authorship.
It should not become a pile of disconnected reward systems.

## Progression Layers Under Consideration

- in-run growth
- loot acquisition
- crafting inputs or upgrade materials
- persistent unlocks across runs
- broader build options unlocked through repeated play

## Constraints

- progression systems must point toward a shared player fantasy
- each added layer must justify its mental overhead
- repeat growth should encourage re-entry without making early runs feel pointless
- crafting should only stay if it adds meaningful choice instead of maintenance busywork

## Reference Notes

Reference genres are used only to identify motivational structures such as compounding runs, retained gains, and build variation.
They are not templates for feature completeness.

## Progression Risks

- loot, crafting, and meta progression may become redundant layers
- persistent growth may trivialize run tension
- progression may feel mandatory rather than expressive
- reward complexity may exceed what the combat loop can meaningfully absorb

## Open Questions

- Which progression layer is the emotional backbone of long-term retention?
- Does crafting add strategy, or just inventory friction?
- What should persist after a failed run?
- How much permanent power is acceptable before runs lose tension?
