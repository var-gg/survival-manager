# Recruitment and Reroll

- Status: draft
- Last Updated: 2026-03-29
- Phase: prototype

## Purpose

This document defines the MVP recruitment and reroll framework.
The goal is to make roster growth and variation meaningful without requiring deep simulation.

## MVP Rules

### Recruitment Role

Recruitment should supply the player with candidate heroes that vary by:

- race
- class
- recruit archetype
- positive trait
- negative trait

Even heroes of the same archetype should be able to fill different roles through trait roll, equipment, and augment combination.

### Recruit Identity Rule

Every recruited hero begins with:

- 1 positive trait
- 1 negative trait

This is mandatory for MVP identity differentiation.

### Reroll Direction

The MVP should include limited reroll support, but not a deep crafting economy.
Reroll should primarily relate to:

- recruitment candidate refresh
- trait reroll currency earned from expedition completion
- gold-based limited improvement or reshaping pressure

### MVP Simplicity Rule

Recruitment should stay readable and avoid excessive layers like lineage trees, training schools, or long-form biography systems.

## Long-Term Expansion Points

- archetype-specific trait pools
- town facilities that influence recruitment pools
- advanced recruit modifiers
- rarity tiers
- PVP-oriented roster sourcing only as a long-term vision, not MVP

## Economic Runaway Risks

- if rerolls are too cheap, recruit identity loses meaning
- if recruit refresh is too abundant, roster pressure disappears
- if gold and reroll currency overlap too much, one economy may dominate the other

## Balance Risks

- strong positive traits may overwhelm class or synergy identity
- harsh negative traits may make many recruits feel like traps
- if same-archetype variance is too small, roster decisions become flat
- if variance is too high, planning becomes noisy rather than strategic

## Data Expansion Points

- recruit archetype definitions
- positive trait catalogs
- negative trait catalogs
- reroll currency definitions
- recruit offer table definitions

## Open Questions

- how many recruit candidates should the player see at one time in MVP?
- should trait reroll target one trait, both traits, or a full recruit refresh?
- what is the minimum reroll pressure needed to keep recruitment interesting?
- how much of recruit identity should come from archetype versus trait roll in the first slice?
