# Roster Cap Recommendation

- Status: draft
- Last Updated: 2026-03-29
- Phase: prototype

## Purpose

This document explains the recommended MVP roster-size caps and the reasoning behind them.

## MVP Recommendation

Lock the following MVP values:

- battle deployment size: 4
- expedition roster size: 8
- town-held roster cap: 12
- races: 3
- classes: 4
- recruit archetypes: 8

## Why 4 / 8 / 12

### Battle Deployment 4

Four deployed units is small enough for readability and balancing, while still allowing formation and synergy decisions.
It supports a wooden-dummy prototype without requiring large-scale encounter tuning.

### Expedition Roster 8

An expedition size of 8 gives room for backup identity, class coverage, and synergy decisions without becoming UI-heavy too early.
It creates a meaningful choice boundary between what comes along and what stays home.

### Town-Held Roster 12

A town-held cap of 12 is large enough to create pressure and replacement decisions, but small enough to manage during MVP.
It supports the feeling of roster curation without demanding full management-game scale.

## Recommended Identity Set

### Races

- Human
- Beastkin
- Undead

### Classes

- Vanguard
- Duelist
- Ranger
- Mystic

## Why This Identity Set

This combination is broad enough to create readable archetypal contrasts:

- Human can anchor baseline flexibility
- Beastkin can emphasize aggression, speed, or feral synergy
- Undead can emphasize attrition, sacrifice, or death-adjacent value
- Vanguard provides frontline stability
- Duelist provides focused offense
- Ranger provides reach or target selection value
- Mystic provides utility, control, or magical support

These are recommendations for MVP proof, not immutable lore commitments.

## Long-Term Direction

Later phases may expand roster caps, add more races/classes, and diversify recruit archetypes.
The MVP should not assume those expansions are already needed.
