# Vision

- Status: draft
- Last Updated: 2026-03-29
- Phase: concept framing

## Purpose

This document captures the current high-level product vision for `survival-manager`.
At this stage, the vision should be treated as a working hypothesis constrained by feasibility, scope, and Steam launch viability.

## Product Hypothesis

`survival-manager` aims to combine:

- a 2.5D or 3D presentation layer
- auto-battle-centric combat with tactical readability
- internally turn-based or tick-based combat resolution that feels continuous to the player
- a tactical setup flavor inspired by squad behavior configuration and positioning logic
- deckmaking as a run-shaping decision layer
- loot, crafting, and repeat-growth meta progression

The target is not a classic SRPG and not a direct-control action game.
The target is a management-heavy strategic auto-battle hybrid where the player wins through preparation, composition, tactical rules, and progression choices.

## Vision Statement

Create a Steam-first strategy auto-battle runner where the player feels smart for setting up the right formation, behavior priorities, and build path rather than for executing high-precision inputs.

## Constraints

- The game must remain understandable in moment-to-moment play.
- The hidden turn/tick logic should support clarity rather than making combat feel opaque.
- Tactical depth should come from strong interactions, not sheer system count.
- Steam release viability matters more than genre-purity.

## Reference Direction

Reference points are for **design vocabulary only**, not for direct imitation.

- auto-battle tactics references: formation value, unit-role clarity, behavior priority setup
- squad management references: preconfigured intent before combat pressure begins
- observer-strategy references: watching plans succeed or fail in readable ways
- deckbuilding references: combinational build direction and run-shaping choice pressure

## Known Concept Tensions

- hidden turn logic and readable player feedback may conflict
- tactical setup depth and run tempo may conflict
- deckbuilding, loot, and crafting can create excessive system load
- 2.5D/3D presentation ambition may outgrow early production capacity

These tensions are currently treated as design risks, not solved assumptions.

## Steam Release Framing

The product should bias toward:

- strong trailer readability
- clear "watch your plan play out" appeal
- understandable store positioning
- manageable content scope for an initial commercial release

## Open Questions

- Is the player managing a single leader plus allies, a full squad, or a hybrid structure?
- How visible should the internal turn/tick logic be to advanced players?
- Is 2.5D sufficient for readability and cost control, or is full 3D necessary?
- How much tactical scripting is enough before usability collapses?
- What is the minimum fun vertical slice that proves the auto-battle management fantasy?
