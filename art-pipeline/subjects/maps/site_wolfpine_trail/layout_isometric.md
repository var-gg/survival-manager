# Wolfpine Trail — layout isometric (cycle 2)

Same scene and same mood as the attached prior output (Wolfpine Trail concept thumbnail) — pine forest path at golden hour, the threshold of an indigenous forest tribe's territory, environment as subject. Match the prior output's identity (warm umber path, sage understory, pine green canopy with golden rim, mossy stone, hazy greenish-grey backdrop, gold sun shafts) and overall mood (peaceful, slightly mysterious).

Cycle stage: map_layout — refine the prior concept toward a CLEANER and MORE READABLE layout where each of the four depth layers is clearly defined, vendor reproduction is more feasible (simpler silhouettes, modular-asset-friendly geometry), and the inner playable path is strictly uncluttered.

## Camera & framing
Quarter-view tilt-down, about 33° elevation, slight 12° rotation, wide cinematic 16:9. Same camera as prior output. The path winds gently from foreground center toward middle distance.

## Four-layer composition (refined from prior)

1. **Inner playable ground (foreground center)** — STRICTLY CLEAN. Only the path itself (warm umber dirt with pine needles) and short edge grass. NO standing stones, NO boulders, NO mid-path stumps, NO scattered totems on the path. The path is the readable action surface — leave it open. The path may have soft texture variation but no geometric props lying on it.

2. **Buffer zone (immediately around the path, BUT NOT ON IT)** — soft natural scatter blending path into forest: short ferns, low painterly grass tufts, mossy rocks, weathered tree stumps placed off the path edge, fallen logs lying parallel to the path off to one side, tucked mushroom clusters near roots. All buffer elements sit OUTSIDE the path edge, decorating the transition into the forest.

3. **Frame zone (LEFT and RIGHT flanks only — proscenium)** — tall stylized pine trees as bold blocky silhouettes on either side. The pine canopies are simple cone-like stylized shapes, NOT individually rendered leaves — think clear silhouette polygon trees with painterly toon shading on top. Their canopies arch slightly inward to frame the stage. Among them, a few large mossy faceted boulders anchor the ground. EXACTLY ONE OR TWO weathered standing stones — dark rough-hewn rectangular shapes with simple flat low-relief carved tribal markings (geometric symbols, NOT illuminated glowing runes) — placed clearly in the frame zone, NEVER in the middle of the path. Frame elements stay at the sides, never crossing into the inner playable area.

4. **Backdrop layer (distant)** — rolling hills covered in simple low-poly pine silhouettes recede into warm hazy distance. Simple layered hill silhouettes, NOT detailed forest-on-forest. Strong atmospheric desaturation toward warm greenish-grey. A few god-ray light shafts cut through from upper-left, catching dust and atmosphere.

## Reproduction feasibility (CRITICAL — improved over cycle 1)

The illustration must look more clearly like a stylized low-poly toon-shaded 3D environment than the prior output. The vendor stack is: simple stylized polygon trees and rocks, modular ruin/standing-stone props, painterly toon shader (cel sparingly at hard edges, painterly gradient elsewhere), painterly post-process for atmosphere.

- Tree silhouettes: simple bold pine cone shapes, clear edge profile, NO individual painted leaves, NO photorealistic bark grain.
- Rocks: bold faceted stylized shapes with stylized moss patches, NOT photoreal micro-detail.
- Standing stones: rectangular rough-hewn slabs with simple flat carved geometric symbols, NOT illuminated runes, NOT intricate gothic carving.
- Foliage: painterly clumps of color, NOT individual grass blades.
- Path: textured warm dirt strip, NOT photoreal soil.
- Hills: simple silhouette layers, fading into haze.
