# Sunken Bastion — concept thumbnail

A half-sunken stone fortress courtyard under heavy overcast sky, the moment after authority has crumbled. Stylized painterly cartoon environment in the tradition of premium mobile JRPG world maps and high-end console anime fantasy game environment splash art — anime-leaning fantasy game aesthetic, NOT photoreal, NOT concept-art realism. Quarter-view tilt-down perspective (about 33° elevation, slight 12° rotation), wide cinematic 16:9. No characters in the scene — environment as subject.

## Scene mood

The viewer stands at the threshold of a fallen kingdom's bastion. The ground has subsided — the entire fortress courtyard tilts gently, with one corner sunk and flooded by a shallow stagnant water layer. Marble pillars lean off-axis. Ivy creeps up cracked outer walls. A few rusted weapons drift in the water. Cool overcast light, no direct sun. The mood is heavy, melancholic, the silent aftermath of a faith that has rotted from within.

## Four-layer composition (near to far)

1. **Inner playable ground (foreground center)** — STRICTLY CLEAN. A flagstone courtyard floor, weathered grey marble tiles with mossy seams, partially submerged in a thin sheet of stagnant water on one side (water rises from the lower-left, covers maybe 20% of the inner area). NO objects on the playable surface — the floor itself is the readable action ground. Soft texture variation (cracked tiles, water reflections) but no props on it.

2. **Buffer zone (around the playable, NOT on it)** — natural decay scatter blending playable into ruin: ivy creeping along stone seams, mossy patches on the flagstones at the edges, a few dropped rusted weapon fragments lying half-submerged in the water section (off-playable), small fallen stone debris chunks pushed to the sides, painterly clumps of grass pushing up through floor cracks at the dry edges.

3. **Frame zone (LEFT and RIGHT flanks only — proscenium)** — tall ruined stone structures framing the stage. On the LEFT: a leaning marble pillar (off-axis from the subsidence) with chipped capital, behind it a partial ruined wall with a broken arched window letting overcast daylight through. On the RIGHT: a ruined keep wall (rectangular stone block construction with weathering and crack lines), a large mossy boulder anchoring the ground, and one weathered stone statue of a robed figure (a sun-priest of the fallen order, robe drape simple, face worn smooth by time) standing slightly forward of the wall. Frame elements stay at the sides, never crossing into the inner playable.

4. **Backdrop layer (distant)** — partially collapsed bastion silhouette behind: a leaning tower with its top half broken off, a longer collapsed keep silhouette beside it, all rendered as simple low-poly silhouettes fading into cool grey haze. Sky is heavy overcast — layered grey clouds with just a hint of pale gold breaking through one seam in the upper-right (suggesting weak filtered daylight, not direct sun). NO god-rays. The backdrop carries the sense of a once-grand fortress now ruined and forgotten.

## Natural basin form
The flooded inner playable sits lowest. Buffer (dry stone tiles, debris, ivy) rises gently around it. Frame zone (pillars, walls, statue, keep) stands taller. Backdrop (collapsed bastion, broken tower) stretches highest into the haze. Eye flows from foreground water → flanking ruin → distant collapsed silhouettes.

## Atmospheric perspective
Foreground: full saturation on stone color, ivy green, rust orange on submerged weapons, cool blue-grey water with subtle reflection.
Middle (frame zone): slightly desaturated, stone darker, ivy duller.
Backdrop: strongly desaturated and fog-tinted toward cool blue-grey, almost monochromatic with hint of pale gold from the sky seam.

## Reproduction feasibility (CRITICAL)
Stylized low-poly toon-shaded 3D environment look — recreatable with simple stylized polygon ruin walls, blocky stone pillars, rectangular keep modules, painterly ivy clusters, faceted boulders, a single stylized robed statue, and a flat transparent water plane.
- Walls: simple block construction with stylized weathering and crack lines, NOT photoreal stone micro-detail, NOT intricate gothic carving.
- Pillars: clean cylinder/capital silhouette with chipped marble texture (painterly), NOT photoreal carved fluting.
- Statue: simple robed humanoid silhouette, face worn smooth, painterly toon shade — NOT a detailed sculpture.
- Water: stylized flat plane with soft reflection, faint ripples, NOT fluid simulation.
- Ivy: painterly leaf clusters draped over edges, NOT individually rendered leaves.
- Distant ruins: simple silhouette layers in haze.

## Palette

- Cool weathered grey marble flagstones
- Mossy green ivy and seam moss
- Rust orange (submerged weapon fragments)
- Cool blue-grey stagnant water with subtle pale reflection
- Warm grey-brown stone walls
- Pale grey overcast sky with hint of pale gold
- Cool desaturated grey-blue backdrop haze

## Camera

- Quarter-view tilt-down, about 33° elevation, slight 12° rotation
- Wide 16:9 cinematic
- Frame proportions: foreground (flagstones + water + buffer) ~50%, frame zone (pillars + walls + statue) ~30%, backdrop (collapsed bastion + sky) ~20%

## What this is NOT

- NOT a character close-up
- NOT photoreal stone or water (this is stylized painterly toon)
- NOT a top-down map view
- NOT a battle screen with HUD / health bars / minimap
- NOT cluttered (playable flagstones + water surface stay clean)
- NOT lens blur on backdrop (atmospheric fade is OK)
- NOT a sun-flooded scene (overcast, NO direct sun, NO god-rays)
- NOT a destroyed warzone with active fire / smoke (this is a quiet aftermath)
